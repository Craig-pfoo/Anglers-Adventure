Rods

Spinning Rod

Spinning rods are among the most popular types of fishing rods due to their versatility and ease of use. They feature a reel mounted on the underside of the rod, allowing for easy casting and retrieval. Spinning rods are suitable for various fishing techniques and can handle a wide range of fish species.


BaitCasting Rod

Baitcasting rods are designed for more precise and controlled casting, making them ideal for targeting larger fish species. They feature a reel mounted on the top side of the rod and require some skill to master casting without backlash. Baitcasting rods often provide greater casting accuracy and sensitivity compared to spinning rods.


Fly Rod

Fly rods are specifically designed for fly fishing, a technique where artificial flies are cast using a weighted line. They are typically longer and more flexible than other types of rods to allow for delicate presentations of the fly. Fly rods come in various weights and lengths, each suited to different types of fly fishing applications and target species.


Surf Rod

Surf rods are designed for fishing in saltwater environments from the shoreline or beach. They are typically longer and sturdier than other rods to enable long casts into the surf and to handle larger fish species commonly found in coastal waters. Surf rods often feature large guides and robust construction to withstand the rigors of casting heavy baits and battling strong currents.


Ice Fishing Rod

They are typically shorter than traditional rods and feature sensitive tips to detect subtle bites in cold water conditions. Ice fishing rods may come in specialized designs such as jigging rods or tip-up rods, each suited to different ice fishing techniques.
