Lures & Bait

When you first go to a place to look for lures and bait it can be very overwhelming. There are so many choices even when just looking for the most basic supplies. This should help with the bare minimum for fishing.

Natural + Artifical Bait

CrankBait

These are hard plastic lures that are usually made to mimic a baitfish. They are used primarily to trigger the fish's natural predatory instincs, these can be used effectivly to sink, float, or hover on the water.

Spinnerbait

A spinnerbait is a popular fishing lure designed to mimic the movement of injured baitfish. It typically consists of a metal wire frame with one or more spinning metal blades attached to one end and a weighted body or jig head at the other. The blades create vibration and flash as they spin through the water, attracting predatory fish like bass, pike, and muskie. Spinnerbaits are versatile and can be used in various fishing conditions, from shallow to deep water, making them a favorite among anglers for both freshwater and saltwater fishing.

Spoons

A spoon bait, also known simply as a spoon, is a type of fishing lure characterized by its shape resembling a spoon. These lures typically consist of a concave, metallic, or reflective body with a single or multiple hooks attached. Spoon baits are designed to imitate the erratic movement of injured baitfish or other prey, making them highly attractive to predatory fish. When retrieved, spoon baits flutter and wobble through the water, creating flash and vibration that entices fish to strike. They are versatile lures suitable for various fishing techniques, including casting, trolling, and jigging, and are effective for targeting a wide range of species, including trout, salmon, bass, and pike.

Jigs

Jigs are versatile fishing lures consisting of a weighted head usually made of lead or tungsten, with one or more hooks attached. The head may be molded into various shapes, often resembling a minnow, crawfish, or other natural prey. Jigs can be dressed with various materials such as feathers, fur, rubber, or soft plastic to enhance their appearance and action in the water.

Flies

Different flies for fishing are specialized artificial lures designed to mimic various aquatic insects, baitfish, crustaceans, or other natural prey species that fish feed on. These flies are primarily used in fly fishing, a distinct angling method that involves casting a lightweight fly line combined with a specialized fly rod and reel.

Dry Flies: These flies are designed to float on the water's surface to imitate adult insects, such as mayflies, caddisflies, or stoneflies. They are typically constructed with buoyant materials like feathers, hair, or foam and are used when fish are feeding on insects at or near the water's surface.
Wet Flies: Unlike dry flies, wet flies are designed to sink below the water's surface. They represent various life stages of aquatic insects, such as nymphs, emergers, or drowned insects. Wet flies are often fished with a slow retrieve or allowed to drift in the current to mimic the natural movement of underwater insects.
Nymphs: Nymphs are imitation larvae or immature stages of aquatic insects that inhabit the stream bottom. They are weighted to sink and are typically fished near the riverbed to imitate the natural behavior of these insects as they drift with the current.
